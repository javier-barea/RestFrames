Frames:

  • Lab Frame: Refers to the lab frame, it stands at the top of the tree. Measures all events that can be measured on the particle accelerator.
  • Decay Frames: Frames of unstable intermediate particles that decay into visible or invisible particles. Information such as mass, variable mass, decay angle, delta-phi can be set. Example particles: Bosons.
  • Visible Frames: Frames of stable visible particles that decay from intermediate particles, and do not decay. Information such as transverse momentum cut, momentum cuts. Example particles: Leptons
  • Invisible Frames: Frames of stable invisible particles that decay from intermediate particles and do not decay. Most of the information is missing. Examples: Neutrinos.

Groups:

  • What are groups?
    • In RestFrames there are two types of groups. Invisible group and Combinatoric group. Invisible group as its name suggests is used to group the invisible particles in a sole place. Combinatoric group is kind of the same but instead of used to group invisible particles it is used to group visible ones.
    • Once we have created a group and have specified the correct and desired frames we can then start to pick the jigsaws we want to use. Note that for invisible group only invisible frames and invisible jigsaws can be added
